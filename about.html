About RealDPI

What is RealDPI?

Even when two mice are set to the same DPI, they can feel completely different in practice. This happens because every sensor is tuned differently — the actual tracking speed varies depending on the mouse model, its firmware, and how the sensor has been calibrated by the manufacturer.

RealDPI solves this problem. Instead of relying on the labeled DPI value, we use real-world measured DPI data to calculate the exact setting you need on your new mouse to match the feel of your old one — so you can switch mice without losing your aim.

Why We Built This

Switching mice has always been a frustrating experience for gamers. Even with the same DPI setting, your muscle memory gets thrown off because the actual sensitivity feels different. Most players spend days re-adjusting — and many never quite get it right.

We built RealDPI to eliminate that friction. Whether you're upgrading to a new mouse or testing different models, you deserve a tool that gives you an accurate, data-driven answer — not a rough estimate.

Data Sources

The DPI measurements used in RealDPI are sourced from the gaming mouse community — including dedicated forums, subreddits such as r/MouseReview, and various enthusiast websites where members have conducted hands-on testing.

All values are based on direct physical measurements performed by community members and reviewed for accuracy before being applied to our calculator. We are grateful to everyone in the community who contributes their time and effort to producing reliable data.
